# HPV Vaccination — Considerations for Clinicians

> [!info] Source
> CDC, for healthcare professionals · Last updated July 9, 2024
> <https://www.cdc.gov/hpv/hcp/vaccination-considerations/index.html>

Related notes: [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] · [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] · [[real-world-safety-of-hpv-vaccines-vaers]]

## Key points

- CDC recommends HPV vaccine for children aged 11–12 years to protect against infections that cause future cancers.
- Vaccination can begin at age 9 and is recommended through age 26 for those not previously vaccinated.

## Introduction

Over 15 years of evidence demonstrates that HPV vaccination provides safe, effective, and long-lasting protection against cancers caused by HPV. Healthcare professionals are parents' most trusted source of information regarding this vaccine.

The CDC encourages providers to recommend HPV vaccination at the same time as other adolescent vaccines.

## Routine recommendations

**Standard schedule**

- Primary recommendation: ages 11–12 years
- Can start as early as age 9
- Catch-up vaccination: ages 13–26 if not previously vaccinated
- Shared decision-making option: ages 27–45

> [!note]
> Vaccination prevents new HPV infections but does not treat existing infections or diseases.

**Dosing schedules**

- **Two doses** for those starting before age 15 (6–12 months apart)
- **Three doses** for those starting at ages 15–26 or immunocompromised individuals

## Vaccine safety

HPV vaccines are very safe. All U.S. vaccines undergo extensive FDA safety testing. The 9-valent vaccine was studied in more than 15,000 males and females during clinical trials.

**Common adverse reactions**

- Local injection-site effects (pain, redness, swelling) in 20–90% of recipients
- Fever (100°F) in 10–13% of recipients
- Systemic reactions like nausea occurred equally in vaccine and placebo groups

**Contraindications**

- Severe allergic reaction to vaccine components
- Yeast hypersensitivity (9-valent vaccine uses baker's yeast)
- Not recommended during pregnancy
