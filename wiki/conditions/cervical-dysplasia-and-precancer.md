# Cervical Dysplasia & Precancer (CIN)

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

## What it is

Persistent high-risk HPV can cause abnormal changes in cervical cells, called **cervical
intraepithelial neoplasia (CIN)** or cervical dysplasia — a **precancer**, not cancer. `[T3]`
[[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] It usually takes **3–7 years** for such cell
changes to develop, and **15–20 years** from infection for cervical cancer to develop if precancer
is untreated. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
Early changes and precancers **rarely cause symptoms**, which is why screening matters. `[T1]`
[[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]

## How it's found and graded

- **Screening** (HPV test ± Pap) flags risk → see [[screening-and-testing]].
- **Colposcopy** with biopsy examines the cervix and grades changes. `[T1]` [[self-collected-vaginal-specimens-for-hpv-testing]]
- Grades: **mild** changes may be watched over time to see if they revert; **moderate/severe (CIN 2 /
  CIN 3)** are treated by removing or destroying the abnormal cells. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]]

## Treatment & after

Treatment for CIN 2/3 removes or ablates abnormal cells (e.g. **LEEP**, excision, freezing). `[T3]`
[[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] The HPV vaccine may help prevent abnormal cells
from recurring after treatment in some adults (CIN 2/3, ages 27–45, healthy immune system). `[T3]`
[[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] See [[hpv-vaccine]].

## Triage after a positive self-collected HPV test `[T1][T2]`

| Result | Recommended next step |
|---|---|
| HPV 16 or 18 positive | **Colposcopy** with cytology + biopsies |
| HPV 45, 33/58, 31, 52, 35/39/68, 51, or pooled "other" (16/18 neg) | Clinician **cytology or dual stain** triage |
| HPV 56/59/66 only | Repeat HPV testing in **1 year** |

Source: Enduring Consensus Committee. `[T2]` [[self-collected-vaginal-specimens-for-hpv-testing]] `[T1]` [[self-collection-for-cervical-cancer-screening]]
