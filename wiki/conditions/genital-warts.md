# Genital Warts

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

## What they are

Genital warts are growths on or around the genitals or anus (and sometimes the cervix/vagina) caused
by **low-risk** HPV types — **most cases by HPV 6 and 11**. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]]
They may itch or cause discomfort, or cause no symptoms. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]]
Low-risk types **do not turn into cancer**. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]] Warts can be
cauliflower-shaped and may also appear in harder-to-see areas. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

## Treatment

There is no cure for the underlying virus, but warts themselves can be treated — see
[[wart-and-lesion-treatment]]. Options include provider-applied or prescription topical medicines,
cryotherapy (freezing), laser, or surgical removal. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
Warts **often recur** after treatment and can be treated again. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
Over-the-counter common-wart removers should **not** be used on genital warts. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]]

## Prevention

The HPV vaccine (Gardasil 9) protects against HPV 6 and 11 and prevents ~90% of genital warts when
given at recommended ages. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] See [[hpv-vaccine]] and
[[transmission-and-prevention]].
