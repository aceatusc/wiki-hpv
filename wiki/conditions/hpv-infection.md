# HPV Infection

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

## What HPV is

HPV (human papillomavirus) is a group of **more than 200 related viruses**; each type has a number.
`[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] HPVs infect only **squamous epithelial cells** on skin and on
moist mucosal surfaces. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] HPV is the **most common sexually
transmitted infection**; most sexually active people acquire it at some point. `[T3]`
[[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]] About **43
million** people in the US are currently infected and ~13 million acquire a new infection each year.
`[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

## Types: cutaneous vs mucosal, low- vs high-risk

- **Cutaneous (skin) types** — cause common warts (hands, feet, arms, legs); not shown to cause
  cancer. `[T3]` [[hpv-for-adults-prevention-testing-and-cancer]]
- **Mucosal (genital/anogenital) types** — ~40 types infect the genitals, anus, and mouth/throat.
  `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] Split into:
  - **Low-risk** (e.g. **HPV 6, 11**) — cause most [[genital-warts]]; rarely cause cancer. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]]
  - **High-risk** (e.g. **HPV 16, 18**, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58) — can evade the immune system, persist,
    and cause [[cervical-dysplasia-and-precancer]] and [[hpv-related-cancers]]. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

## Natural history: usually cleared, sometimes persistent

In **~90%** of people the immune system controls or clears HPV, often within **1–2 years**, without
lasting consequences. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]]
A minority of high-risk infections **persist**; persistence is what carries cancer risk over 15–20
years. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] See [[clearance-and-persistence]] for the
persistence picture and why "clearance" is hard to pin down.

## Symptoms

Usually **none** — most people never know they have HPV. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]]
Possible signs depend on type and site: cutaneous warts; genital warts (low-risk mucosal); or, much
later, the cell changes/cancers caused by persistent high-risk types. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
People with weakened immune systems (e.g. HIV) are at higher risk of HPV-related problems. `[T3]`
[[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

## "Is there a cure?"

> [!warning] Tier conflict — see [[synthesis]] #1
> There is **no treatment for the HPV infection itself** — only for what it causes (warts,
> precancers, cancers). `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]] `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
> Many patients report supplements "cleared" their HPV `[T4]` [[clearance-anecdotes]] — recorded as
> patient-reported and consistent with natural clearance, **not** established as a cure. Defer to T1.
