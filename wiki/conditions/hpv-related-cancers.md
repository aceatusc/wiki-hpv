# HPV-Related Cancers

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

Persistent infection with high-risk HPV (chiefly **16 and 18**) causes six cancer types. More than
**39,000** HPV-related cancers occur in the US each year; the HPV vaccine can prevent **>90%** of
them when given at recommended ages. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] Globally, HPV caused ~620,000
cancers in women and ~70,000 in men in 2019. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]

## The six cancers

- **Cervical** — the most common HPV cancer in people with a cervix; **nearly all** cervical cancers
  are caused by HPV. Preventable with vaccination + screening. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
  `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] It is the **only** HPV cancer with a screening test.
  `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] See [[screening-and-testing]].
- **Oropharyngeal (mouth/throat)** — most cancers in the back of the throat are HPV-related; these
  are the **most common HPV-related cancers in men**. No standard screening test. `[T3]` [[hpv-for-adults-prevention-testing-and-cancer]]
- **Anal** — in both sexes; more common in people with HIV and men who have sex with men. Anal
  cytology ("anal Pap") is suggested for some higher-risk groups, not routine. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
- **Penile** — more common in men with HIV and MSM; no standard screening. `[T3]` [[hpv-for-adults-prevention-testing-and-cancer]]
- **Vulvar** and **Vaginal** — less common; vaginal precancers may be found with cervical screening;
  no standard vulvar screening. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

## Timeline & prevention

Cervical cancer typically develops **15–20 years** after infection (faster with weakened immunity),
via [[cervical-dysplasia-and-precancer]]. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] Prevention rests
on two pillars: [[hpv-vaccine]] and [[screening-and-testing]]. WHO's elimination targets: 90%
vaccinated by 15, 70% screened with high-performance tests by 35 and 45, 90% with disease treated.
`[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
