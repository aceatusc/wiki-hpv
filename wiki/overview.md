# HPV Knowledge Wiki — Overview

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care.
> This wiki organizes what the group has read about HPV. It does not diagnose, treat, or advise.
> Anything that would change what you do — screening, treatment, disclosure, symptoms — belongs in a
> conversation with a qualified clinician.

## Evidence tiers (how to read this wiki)

Every claim carries a tier marker so you can see what it rests on:

- `[T1]` — guidelines & synthesized evidence (CDC, ACS, ACOG, ASCCP, WHO, USPSTF)
- `[T2]` — primary research (studies, surveillance)
- `[T3]` — expert/editorial & patient-education references
- `[T4]` — **patient-reported / anecdotal** (Reddit) — never established fact; deferred to higher tiers on conflict

## Current evidence snapshot

- **HPV is extremely common and usually self-limiting.** Most sexually active people get HPV; in
  ~90% of cases the immune system controls or clears it, often within 1–2 years. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
- **A minority of infections persist and matter.** Persistent high-risk HPV (esp. types 16/18) can,
  over 15–20 years, cause cervical and other cancers. `[T1]` [[hpv-related-cancers]]
- **There is no treatment for the HPV infection itself** — only for what it causes (warts,
  precancers, cancers). `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]] This is the single most common point of
  confusion in the patient discussions. [[clearance-and-persistence]]
- **Two interventions do the heavy lifting:** the HPV vaccine (Gardasil 9 in the US; prevents >90% of
  HPV cancers when given at recommended ages) `[T1]` [[hpv-vaccine]] and routine cervical screening,
  now including self-collected HPV testing. `[T1]` [[screening-and-testing]]
- **The vaccine's real-world safety profile is favorable.** An 18-year analysis of 76,575 US VAERS
  reports found most events were mild vasovagal reactions and no signal for death, GBS, or
  autoimmune disease. `[T2]` [[real-world-safety-of-hpv-vaccines-vaers]]
- **The lived experience is heavy and under-served by the clinical material.** Patient discussions
  are dominated by anxiety, stigma, partner-disclosure dilemmas, and unverified "cures." These are
  recorded as patient-reported, clearly separated from the evidence. `[T4]` [[psychological-impact]]

## How to contribute

1. Add a source to the right `raw/` tier folder.
2. Ask the agent to **ingest** it (see [[CLAUDE]] §4).
3. Review the change — especially anything treatment-relevant.

> [!note] If you are here because of your own diagnosis
> HPV is common and usually clears on its own; a positive result is not a cancer diagnosis. But this
> wiki cannot tell you what to do about your situation — please talk to a clinician. If you are in
> distress, see the support note on [[psychological-impact]].
