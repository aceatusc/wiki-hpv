# Running Synthesis & Conflict Register

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

This page holds (a) the cross-tier synthesis — where the sources agree and reinforce — and (b) the
**conflict register**, where tiers disagree. Conflicts are resolved by deferring to the higher tier
while still recording the lower-tier claim.

## Where the tiers agree (strong, multi-tier)

- **HPV is common and usually clears.** Stated consistently across T1 (WHO, ACS), T3 (ACOG, Planned
  Parenthood), and echoed in T4 ("most people clear it in 2 years"). `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]]
- **Vaccinate early; screen regardless of vaccination.** T1 (CDC, ACS, ACOG) and T3 align: vaccinate
  at 9–12, and people with a cervix still need screening even if vaccinated. `[T1]` [[hpv-vaccine]] [[screening-and-testing]]
- **Self-collected HPV testing is accurate and expands access.** T1 guideline (ASCCP/Enduring
  Consensus) and T2 study agree, with the caveat that PCR assays match clinician collection while
  mRNA/signal-amplification assays are less sensitive on self-samples. `[T1][T2]` [[screening-and-testing]]
- **Vaccine safety is reassuring.** T1 guidance ("very safe") is corroborated by a T2 18-year VAERS
  analysis (no death/GBS/autoimmune signal). `[T1][T2]` [[real-world-safety-of-hpv-vaccines-vaers]]

## Where the tiers diverge — Conflict Register

> [!warning] Tier conflict #1 — "supplements / regimens cure HPV"
> **T4 (patient-reported):** Many r/HPV users credit specific regimens for clearing persistent HPV —
> most prominently **Pervistop** (EGCG + folic acid + B12 + hyaluronic acid), and also AHCC (e.g.
> "kinoko"), Papilocare, turkey tail, echinacea, and immune/"lifestyle" boosting. `[T4]` [[clearance-anecdotes]] [[supplements-and-alternative-approaches]]
> **T1 (guidelines):** *There is no treatment for the HPV infection itself*; most infections are
> controlled/cleared by the immune system within 1–2 years regardless of intervention. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]] [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
> **Resolution:** Recorded as patient-reported experience, **not** established as effective. Reported
> clearance is fully consistent with the expected natural history (and with concurrent vaccination,
> which several posters also did). The underlying Pervistop literature is small/single-arm. Defer to T1.

> [!warning] Tier conflict #2 — "you must disclose / there's a ping-pong reinfection risk"
> **T4 (patient-reported):** Conflicting advice abounds — some users insist on disclosing active
> high-risk HPV to every partner and fear continuous "ping-pong" reinfection; others (including some
> clinicians quoted) say routine male testing is not useful and reinfection with one's own type is
> low-risk. `[T4]` [[partner-disclosure-and-relationships]]
> **T1/T3:** There is no FDA-approved HPV test for men/general "HPV status"; HPV testing is a
> cervical *cancer screening* tool, not an STI status test. Re-infection with the *same* type you
> already have is low-risk. `[T1]` [[screening-and-testing]] `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]]
> **Resolution:** Disclosure is a personal/relational choice, not a clinical mandate the sources
> establish; the wiki records the lived dilemma without prescribing. Defer to T1/T3 on the testing facts.

> [!warning] Tier conflict #3 — "HPV is a catastrophe / where is the cure"
> **T4 (patient-reported):** Diagnosis frequently triggers catastrophic thinking — life "ruined,"
> relationships doomed, even suicidal ideation. `[T4]` [[psychological-impact]]
> **T1/T3:** For most people HPV is asymptomatic and self-limiting; a positive test is not a cancer
> diagnosis. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
> **Resolution:** The emotional reality is real and recorded; the catastrophic *beliefs* are
> contradicted by the evidence and gently corrected on the patient-experience pages.

## Open gaps (for Lint / future sourcing)

  mentions — a targeted T1/T2 source would strengthen [[hpv-related-cancers]].
- **Male HPV** evidence is thin across all tiers; T4 demand is high ([[men-and-hpv]]). Worth seeking
  T1/T2 sources.
- The **Pervistop / EGCG** primary literature (Aragona et al., *J Clin Med* 2023) is referenced only
  via T4; ingesting the actual paper as T2 would let the wiki weight conflict #1 directly.
- No source yet on **HPV natural history / clearance kinetics** as a primary study; would anchor
  [[clearance-and-persistence]] beyond guideline summaries.
