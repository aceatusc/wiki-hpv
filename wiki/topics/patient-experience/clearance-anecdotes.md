# Patient Experience — Clearance Anecdotes

> [!warning] Tier-4 / patient-reported — not medical advice and not established fact.
> This page collects **what people say worked**. It does **not** establish that anything did. For the
> evidence evaluation, see [[supplements-and-alternative-approaches]] and [[synthesis]] #1.

## The theme

"I cleared it" posts are among the most-engaged in r/HPV and serve a real function: **hope** during
the "wait and see" of [[clearance-and-persistence]]. They typically pair an emotional relief story
with a list of supplements/lifestyle changes the poster credits. Representative:

## What's credited (patient-reported, not endorsed)

Pervistop / EGCG + folic acid + B12 + hyaluronic acid; AHCC; turkey tail; Papilocare; echinacea;
zinc, vitamin D, ashwagandha; "immune boosting" and lifestyle change. Itemized with the safety notes
on [[supplements-and-alternative-approaches]].

## Why these stories are not evidence

> [!warning] Tier conflict (see [[synthesis]] #1)
> Most HPV **clears on its own** within 1–2 years. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] So a
> person who takes *anything* and later retests negative will usually attribute clearance to it —
> classic confounding by natural history, frequent **co-vaccination**, weak primary literature, and
> survivorship in who posts. **Resolution:** recorded as hope and lived experience; **not**
> established as effective. Defer to T1.

## Why we keep them anyway

These threads are the clearest signal of an **unmet need** — patients with persistent HPV want
something to *do*. That's a real, recordable demand (and a prompt to seek stronger sources on
clearance, per [[synthesis]] → open gaps), even though the remedies themselves are unproven.
