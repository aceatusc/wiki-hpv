# Transmission & Prevention

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

## How HPV spreads

Genital HPV spreads by **intimate skin-to-skin contact** — vaginal, anal, and oral sex — and can be
passed **even with no symptoms** and even without penetration or ejaculation. `[T3]`
[[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]] It is **not** spread by blood/body
fluids, and **not** by toilet seats, hugging, pools/hot tubs, sharing food/utensils, or being
"unclean." `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] You can have HPV years after last sexual activity.
`[T3]` [[hpv-for-adults-prevention-testing-and-cancer]] Rarely, a mother can transmit HPV to a
newborn during birth (respiratory papillomatosis). `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

## Lowering risk

- **Vaccination** — the single most effective preventive measure. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] See [[hpv-vaccine]].
- **Condoms / dental dams** — reduce but do **not** eliminate risk (uncovered skin can transmit);
  use correctly every time. `[T3]` [[hpv-for-adults-prevention-testing-and-cancer]]
- **Fewer partners / partner history** lowers exposure odds. `[T3]` [[hpv-for-adults-prevention-testing-and-cancer]]
- **Not smoking** and (per WHO) voluntary male circumcision reduce persistence/acquisition risk.
  `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
- **Screening** doesn't prevent infection but prevents cancer by catching precancers early. `[T1]`
  [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] See [[screening-and-testing]].

## "Who gave it to whom?"

Because HPV can stay dormant/undetected for years, a new diagnosis **cannot** date the infection or
identify a source — a recurring source of relationship conflict. `[T3]` [[hpv-for-adults-prevention-testing-and-cancer]]
See [[partner-disclosure-and-relationships]].
