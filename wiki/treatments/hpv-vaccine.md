# HPV Vaccine (Gardasil 9)

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

## What it does

The HPV vaccine **prevents** new infection with the HPV types that cause most HPV cancers and most
genital warts. It does **not** treat an existing infection or disease. `[T1]` [[hpv-vaccine-recommendations]]
`[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] **Gardasil 9** is the only HPV vaccine available in the US; it
covers low-risk **6, 11** and high-risk **16, 18, 31, 33, 45, 52, 58**. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
Given at recommended ages it prevents **>90%** of HPV-related cancers and ~90% of genital warts.
`[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] It contains no live virus or viral DNA, so it cannot cause HPV.
`[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]

## Who and when

- **Routine: ages 11–12** (can start at **9**). `[T1]` [[hpv-vaccine-recommendations]]
- **Catch-up: 13–26** if not fully vaccinated. `[T1]` [[hpv-vaccine-recommendations]]
- **27–45: shared clinical decision-making** (CDC/ACOG; ACS does *not* recommend routinely >26).
  `[T1]` [[hpv-vaccine-recommendations]] `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

**Dosing:** 2 doses if starting before 15 (6–12 months apart); 3 doses if starting at 15–26 or
immunocompromised. `[T1]` [[hpv-vaccine-recommendations]] Missed doses don't require restarting the
series. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] Not given in pregnancy. `[T1]`
[[hpv-vaccine-recommendations]]

## Safety `[T1][T2]`

Considered **very safe** after extensive testing and hundreds of millions of doses. Common reactions
are local (pain/redness/swelling), sometimes fever; serious reactions (e.g. allergy, fainting) are
rare. `[T1]` [[hpv-vaccine-recommendations]] `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

> [!info] Corroborating primary evidence `[T2]`
> An 18-year analysis of **76,575** US VAERS reports found serious-event proportions **fell** across
> generations (Cervarix 33% → Gardasil 16% → Gardasil 9 ~8%); most events were acute vasovagal
> reactions; **no signal** for death, Guillain–Barré, or autoimmune/demyelinating disease; strongest
> signals were preventable administration errors. A 15-minute post-vaccination observation period is
> advised. `[T2]` [[real-world-safety-of-hpv-vaccines-vaers]]

## Still screen after vaccination

Vaccination does not cover every oncogenic type, so people with a cervix **still need cervical
screening**. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] See [[screening-and-testing]].
