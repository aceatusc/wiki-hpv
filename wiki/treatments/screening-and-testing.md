# Screening & Testing

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

## HPV test vs Pap test

- **HPV test** looks for high-risk HPV infection in the cervix; it cannot detect precancer/cancer
  itself. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
- **Pap test** looks for abnormal cervical cells; it cannot detect HPV. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
- **Primary HPV test** = HPV test alone; **co-test** = HPV + Pap together. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
- Some HPV assays detect **mRNA E6/E7** (the oncogenic viral proteins' instructions); others detect
  HPV **DNA**. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

HPV testing is **only FDA-approved for cervical screening** — there is no approved general "HPV
status" test, and results can change over months/years. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

## ACS screening schedule `[T1/T3]`

Average-risk people with a cervix: **start at 25, continue to at least 65**: `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]

- Primary HPV test on a **provider-collected cervical** sample every **5 years** (preferred)
- Primary HPV test on a **self-collected vaginal** sample every **3 years**
- **Co-test** every 5 years
- Pap alone every 3 years if HPV testing unavailable

Screen even if vaccinated, in a long-term relationship, not sexually active, or in menopause. `[T3]`
[[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] (ACOG notes screening start at 21 in some guidance — guidelines vary by
body. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]])

## Self-collection `[T1][T2]`

Self-collected **vaginal** HPV testing expands access for unscreened/underscreened people and is
**as accurate as clinician collection when PCR (target-amplification) assays are used**; mRNA/signal-
amplification assays are **less sensitive** on self-samples. `[T1]` [[self-collection-for-cervical-cancer-screening]]
`[T2]` [[self-collected-vaginal-specimens-for-hpv-testing]]

- **Clinician-collected cervical samples are preferred**; self-collected vaginal samples are
  **acceptable** to improve access. `[T1]` [[self-collection-for-cervical-cancer-screening]]
- Eligibility: asymptomatic, **average-risk, routine screening** only — **not** for surveillance
  after abnormal results/colposcopy/treatment, not if symptomatic, HIV+, DES-exposed, or ages 21–24.
  `[T1]` [[self-collection-for-cervical-cancer-screening]]
- A **positive** self-collected test → see a provider for a cervical sample / colposcopy. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]]
- FDA-approved platforms include Roche cobas, BD Onclarity, Abbott Alinity (in healthcare settings)
  and the **Teal Wand** for at-home average-risk 25–65. `[T1]` [[self-collection-for-cervical-cancer-screening]]

### Triage after a positive self-collected HPV result `[T2]`

| Result | Next step |
|---|---|
| HPV-negative | Repeat in **3 years** |
| HPV 16 or 18 | **Colposcopy** + cytology + biopsies |
| HPV 45, 33/58, 31, 52, 35/39/68, 51, or pooled "other" (16/18 neg) | Cytology/dual-stain triage |
| HPV 56/59/66 only | Repeat HPV testing in **1 year** |

Source: Enduring Consensus Committee. `[T2]` [[self-collected-vaginal-specimens-for-hpv-testing]]

## Testing in men / other sites

There is **no routine, clinically useful HPV test for men** or for general STI-status purposes; HPV
testing is a **cancer-screening** tool. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] See [[men-and-hpv]].
