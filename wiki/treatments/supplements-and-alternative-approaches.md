# Supplements & Alternative Approaches

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].
> This page evaluates **patient-reported** remedies against the evidence. It is the clearest example
> in this wiki of a tier-4-vs-tier-1 conflict. Read the resolution boxes, not just the lists.

## The claim (tier-4)

A large share of r/HPV's most-engaged threads are people reporting that a supplement regimen
"cleared" their persistent HPV. The recurring items: `[T4]` [[clearance-anecdotes]]

- **Pervistop** / its components taken separately: **EGCG** (green-tea extract) + **folic acid** +
  **vitamin B12** + **hyaluronic acid** — the most-cited regimen, e.g.
  [[i-cleared-my-persistent-hpv-this-is-what-i-personally-did]]
- **AHCC** (mushroom extract, e.g. "kinoko"); **turkey tail**; **Papilocare** (vaginal gel);
  **echinacea**; zinc, vitamin D, ashwagandha, "immune boosting" and lifestyle changes
- Framed as: take for ~3 months, retest, and credit the supplement if negative

## The evidence (tier-1)

> [!warning] Tier conflict — supplement "cures" (see [[synthesis]] #1)
> **T1 (guidelines):** There is **no treatment for the HPV infection itself**. `[T1]`
> [[human-papillomavirus-hpv]] `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] Most infections are
> controlled or cleared by the **immune system within 1–2 years** regardless of intervention. `[T1]`
> [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
> **Why the anecdotes look convincing anyway:**
> 1. **Base-rate clearance** — most HPV clears on its own, so anyone who waits and retests is likely
>    to turn negative whether or not they took anything.
> 2. **Co-interventions** — several posters also got **vaccinated** around the same time and credit
>    the supplement; cause is unattributable. `[T4]` [[i-cleared-my-persistent-hpv-this-is-what-i-personally-did]]
> 3. **Weak primary literature** — the Pervistop signal traces to a small, single-arm study
>    (Aragona et al., *J Clin Med* 2023, ~20 women); not yet ingested here as T2. See [[synthesis]]
>    open gaps.
> 4. **Selection/survivorship** — people who clear post for "hope"; those who don't, often don't.
> **Resolution:** Recorded as patient-reported experience, **not** established as effective. Defer to
> T1. Reported clearance is consistent with natural history.

## Safety notes worth recording (still not advice)

- **EGCG / high-dose green-tea extract** can cause GI upset and, at high doses, **liver injury** —
  raised by users themselves. `[T4]` [[i-cleared-my-persistent-hpv-this-is-what-i-personally-did]]
- Supplements are **not a substitute** for indicated treatment (e.g. LEEP for CIN 2/3) and should
  not delay it. `[T1/T3]` [[wart-and-lesion-treatment]]
