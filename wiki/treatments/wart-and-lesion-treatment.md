# Wart & Lesion Treatment

> [!warning] Not medical advice — synthesis of sources, not a substitute for clinical care. See [[overview]].

The HPV virus itself cannot be cured, but the things it causes **can** be treated. `[T3]`
[[hpv-for-adults-prevention-testing-and-cancer]] Treatment targets warts, precancerous cell changes,
and cancers — not the infection.

## Genital warts

Treated by a provider with prescription/topical medicines, **cryotherapy** (freezing), laser, or
surgical removal. `[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] Warts **often recur** and can be re-treated.
`[T3]` [[hpv-human-papillomavirus]] Do **not** use pharmacy common-wart removers on genital warts.
`[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] See [[genital-warts]].

## Precancers (cervical and other)

Cervical precancers ([[cervical-dysplasia-and-precancer]], CIN) are removed or destroyed before they
can become cancer — e.g. **LEEP**, excision, or ablation (freezing/heating). `[T3]`
[[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]] `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]]
Precancerous lesions of the vulva, vagina, anus, or penis are similarly treated by ablation or
surgery. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] The HPV vaccine may reduce recurrence after CIN
2/3 treatment in eligible adults. `[T3]` [[human-papillomavirus-infection-and-vaccination]]

## Cancers

HPV-related cancers are managed per oncology guidelines (surgery, radiotherapy, systemic therapy)
and respond best when found **early**. `[T1]` [[human-papillomavirus-and-cancer]] See [[hpv-related-cancers]].

## Patient-reported experience `[T4]`
